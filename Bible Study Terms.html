🗓️ Field Service Meeting Outline

Theme: Adjusting Our Language to Reach the Heart

Focus: Using updated, friendlier expressions in our ministry

Based on: September 2025 Watchtower – “'Rightly Disposed' Ones Will Respond”

Introduction (1 minute)

Today, we’ll focus on just one aspect of how our approach in the ministry is evolving: the words we use.

Specifically, we’ll talk about replacing some of the old terms that might feel formal or intimidating with language that feels natural and welcoming.

Main Point (2 minutes)

Category Old Way New Way
Common Terms “Bible study,” “Bible course,” “Teach you” “Discussion,” “Get to know the Bible”

Using expressions like “Bible study” or “Bible course” may unintentionally sound like a class or commitment. And “Teach you” can feel one-sided or formal.

The new approach encourages terms like:

  • “Would you like to have a Bible discussion?”
  • “We help people get to know the Bible.”

These expressions feel more like an open invitation—not an obligation.

Demonstration (Optional – 2 minutes)

Old Way Example

“Hi, we’re offering a free Bible study. Would you like to join our Bible course?”

New Way Example

“Hi, we’re sharing a thought from the Bible today. A lot of people enjoy short discussions that help them get to know the Bible better.”

Notice the second example sounds friendlier, and removes the feeling of pressure.

Conclusion (1 minute)

Jehovah draws people who are “rightly disposed.” (Acts 13:48) Our goal is to invite—not push.

By simply adjusting the terms we use, we make it easier for someone to say “yes” to learning about God.
